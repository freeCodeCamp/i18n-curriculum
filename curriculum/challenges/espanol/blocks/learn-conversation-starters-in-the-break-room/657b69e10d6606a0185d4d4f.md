# --description--

Hasta ahora has aprendido a crear preguntas con el verbo `to be`.  In sentences with the verb `to be` (`am`, `is`, `are`) you just have to change the order of the noun and the verb to create a question. De esta forma:

| Afirmativa | Interrogativa |
| --- | --- |
| `You are a developer.` | `Are you a developer?` |

Changing the order of the noun and the verb doesn't work in every situation. La mayoría de las frases necesitan que añadas un verbo auxiliar para crear preguntas. Uno de los verbos auxiliares más comunes es `do`.

Puedes usar el verbo `do` como un auxiliar para la mayoría de verbos.  In the present tense it assumes the form of `do` (for `I`, `you`, `we`, `they`) and `does` (for `he`, `she`, `it`).  It is like a marker you will add to the beginning of your sentence; this will let everyone know that sentence is a question. Por ejemplo:

| Afirmativa | Interrogativa |
| --- | --- |
| `You debug on Fridays.` | `Do you debug on Fridays?` |
